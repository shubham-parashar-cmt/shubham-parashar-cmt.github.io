Research project

Tip-induced nonlinear screening and capacitance response in graphene

Manuscript in preparation

This project studies nonlinear Thomas–Fermi screening of graphene by a local electrostatic tip. The goal is to connect simulated charge profiles and induced potentials to measurable response functions.

Current focus

  • Nonlinear electrostatic response to a finite-height cone/tip geometry.
  • Response radii, induced charge, and capacitance/compressibility proxies.
  • Separation of robust observables from geometry-specific simulation artifacts.

Public notes and code will be added after the working notes are cleaned.
