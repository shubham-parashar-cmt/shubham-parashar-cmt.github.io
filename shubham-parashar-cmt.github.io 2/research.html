Research overview

My work is centered on theory for spatially inhomogeneous electronic transport in two-dimensional systems. The common thread is to connect microscopic or mesoscopic models to observables measured in transport and scanning-probe experiments.

Transport near inhomogeneities

Magnetotransport around density perturbations in two-dimensional electron gases, including diffusive, hydrodynamic, and crossover regimes. A recurring theme is how local density structure reshapes electrochemical-potential and current-flow patterns over scales larger than the bare perturbation.

Scanning-probe response

Theoretical interpretation of scanning tunneling potentiometry and local electrostatics in graphene heterostructures, especially around circular p–n junctions and tip-induced potential landscapes.

Nonlinear screening and capacitance

Nonlinear Thomas–Fermi screening near local gates and STM tips, with emphasis on experimentally meaningful response functions such as local capacitance, compressibility, and induced charge profiles.

Quantum Hall and correlated regimes

Spin splitting, thermodynamic response, and quantum Hall transport in two-dimensional electron systems, with a longer-term interest in correlated and topological phases.

Representative methods

Analytical asymptotics; hydrodynamic and kinetic equations; semiclassical Boltzmann transport; electrostatics and Thomas–Fermi theory; numerical solvers; parameter scans; scientific visualization; LaTeX and reproducible computational workflows.
