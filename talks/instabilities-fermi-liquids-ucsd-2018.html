Archival talk

Instabilities in Fermi Liquids

UC San Diego Physics Colloquium, San Diego, CA, 2018.

Summary

This presentation reviewed how renormalization-group ideas diagnose instabilities of Fermi liquids, with emphasis on two routes toward non-Fermi-liquid behavior: van Hove singularities and Pomeranchuk/nematic instabilities.

The first part contrasted one-dimensional and two-dimensional weak-coupling RG logic, then discussed square-lattice van Hove points, nesting, and marginal-Fermi-liquid-like damping from singular low-energy phase space. The second part reviewed nematic order parameters, electron liquid-crystal phases, Landau parameters, Hubbard-Stratonovich decoupling of interaction channels, collective bosonic modes, and the induced fermion self-energy near nematic quantum criticality.
