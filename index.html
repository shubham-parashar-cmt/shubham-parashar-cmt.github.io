About

I study quantum Hall physics in higher Landau levels, magneto-transport in high magnetic fields, and itinerant phases in correlated two-dimensional systems. I also explore in-spiral black hole mergers in D > 5 dimensions.

Research

Research Themes

  • Hydrodynamics & Turbulence — DNS of 2+1D advection; renormalization-group scaling laws for turbulent flows.
  • Correlated Electrons & Transport — Marginal Fermi liquids in 2D; symplectic Sp(2N) Hertz–Millis theory; non-associative operator structures.
  • Non-Hermitian & Lattice Theory — Extensions of the Nielsen–Ninomiya no-go constraints to non-Hermitian settings.
  • Topological Phases — Quantum double models in 3+1D.
  • Gravitation & HEP — Higher-dimensional (D > 5) black-hole inspirals; CP violation in SUSY Pati–Salam / SO(10) frameworks.

Selected Work

  • DNS of turbulent advection (2+1D): direct numerical simulation and scaling diagnostics for advective transport.
  • RG for turbulence: renormalization-group derivations and tests of scaling laws.
  • QMC for adiabatic processes: quantum Monte Carlo codes and benchmarks — MEXT Fellow, JAIST (Ryo Maezono), 2015–2016.
  • Marginal Fermi liquids in 2D: transport signatures and phenomenology.
  • Non-associative operators in condensed matter: algebraic structures and observables.
  • Symplectic Sp(2N) Hertz–Millis: correlated itinerant phases in multi-component fermion systems.
  • Non-Hermitian lattice fermions: reshaping Nielsen–Ninomiya constraints.
  • SUSY Pati–Salam / SO(10) CP violation: model building and CP-violating sectors.
  • Black-hole inspirals in D > 5: dynamics and thermodynamic aspects beyond 3+1D.
  • Quantum double models (3+1D): excitations and constraints for higher-dimensional topological order.
