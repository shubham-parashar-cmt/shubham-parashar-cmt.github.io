Theoretical condensed matter physics

Quantum transport in two-dimensional electronic systems.

I study magnetotransport, electrostatics, hydrodynamics, and scanning-probe response in graphene and related two-dimensional electron systems.

Research profile

I am a theoretical condensed-matter physicist trained at UC San Diego. My recent work develops kinetic, hydrodynamic, electrostatic, and quantum Hall descriptions of inhomogeneous two-dimensional conductors, with emphasis on experimentally interpretable nanoscale imaging observables.
