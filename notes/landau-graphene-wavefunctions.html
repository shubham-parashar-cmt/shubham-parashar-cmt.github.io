Landau Green's functions and graphene wave functions

A cleaned calculation note collecting Landau-level Green's functions, monolayer graphene Green's functions, Born approximation setup, and partial-wave spinors for circular graphene scattering.

PDF TeX source

Nonrelativistic Landau-level Green's function

For a two-dimensional electron in a perpendicular magnetic field and Landau gauge \(\mathbf A=(-By,0)\),

\[ \hat H_e=\frac{\hat{\boldsymbol\pi}^2}{2m},\qquad \hat{\boldsymbol\pi}=\hat{\mathbf p}+e\mathbf A, \qquad L=\sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{eB}}. \]

The eigenstates may be written as

\[ \psi^e_{n k_x}(\boldsymbol\rho)=\frac{e^{i k_xx}}{\sqrt{2\pi}}\varphi_n(\xi), \qquad \xi=\frac{y}{L}-k_xL, \]

with

\[ \varphi_n(\xi)=\frac{1}{\sqrt{L2^n n!\sqrt\pi}}H_n(\xi)e^{-\xi^2/2}, \qquad E_n=\hbar\omega_c\left(n+\frac12\right). \]

Using \(G_e=(H_e-E)^{-1}\), the spectral representation is

\[ G_e(\boldsymbol\rho,\boldsymbol\rho',E)= \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{e^{ik_x(x-x')}\varphi_n(\xi)\varphi_n(\xi')} {2\pi[\hbar\omega_c(n+1/2)-E]}\,dk_x. \]

After the Gaussian-Hermite integral,

\[ G_e=\frac{e^{-R^2/2+i\chi}}{2\pi\hbar\omega_cL^2} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{L_n(R^2)}{n+1/2-\mathcal E}, \qquad \mathcal E=\frac{E}{\hbar\omega_c}, \]

where

\[ R^2=\frac{|\boldsymbol\rho-\boldsymbol\rho'|^2}{2L^2}, \qquad \chi=\frac{(x-x')(y+y')}{2L^2}. \]

The Laguerre sum gives the Whittaker form

\[ \boxed{ G_e(\boldsymbol\rho,\boldsymbol\rho',E)= \frac{e^{i\chi}}{2\pi\hbar\omega_cL^2|R|} \Gamma\!\left(\frac12-\mathcal E\right)W_{\mathcal E,0}(R^2)}. \]

Monolayer graphene Green's function

At one valley of monolayer graphene,

\[ H_M=u(\sigma_x\pi_x+\sigma_y\pi_y),\qquad \omega=\frac{\sqrt2 u}{L}. \]

The Landau levels are

\[ E_{ns}=s\hbar\omega\sqrt n, \qquad s=\pm1, \]

with spinors

\[ \psi^M_{n k_xs}(\boldsymbol\rho)= \frac{e^{ik_xx}}{\sqrt{2\pi(2-\delta_{n0})}} \begin{pmatrix}-s\varphi_{n-1}(\xi)\\ \varphi_n(\xi)\end{pmatrix}. \]

Let \(\bar E=E/(\hbar\omega)\). The diagonal components reduce to

\[ G^M_{\sigma\sigma}=\frac{\bar E e^{-R^2/2+i\chi}}{2\pi\hbar\omega L^2} \sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\frac{L_n(R^2)}{n+\sigma-\bar E^2},\qquad \sigma=0,1. \]

Thus

\[ G^M_{00}=\frac{\bar E e^{i\chi}}{2\pi\hbar\omega L^2|R|} \Gamma(-\bar E^2)W_{\bar E^2+1/2,0}(R^2), \]

\[ G^M_{11}=\frac{\bar E e^{i\chi}}{2\pi\hbar\omega L^2|R|} \Gamma(1-\bar E^2)W_{\bar E^2-1/2,0}(R^2). \]

The off-diagonal components are obtained from the Laguerre identity

\[ L_{n-1}^{1}(R^2)=\frac{n}{R^2}\left[L_{n-1}(R^2)-L_n(R^2)\right], \]

which gives

\[ G^M_{\sigma,1-\sigma}=\frac{r_{\sigma,1-\sigma}\bar E}{R^2} \left(G^M_{11}-G^M_{00}\right). \]

Born approximation

For a weak scalar potential \(\widetilde V(\mathbf r)\), the Born wave function is

\[ \Psi(\mathbf r)=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\begin{pmatrix}1\\1\end{pmatrix}e^{ikx} +\int G^M(\mathbf r,\mathbf r';E)\widetilde V(\mathbf r')\Psi(\mathbf r')d^2r'. \]

Massive Dirac spinors in symmetric gauge

For

\[ H=v_F\boldsymbol\sigma\cdot(-i\hbar\boldsymbol\nabla-e\mathbf A)+\Delta\sigma_z, \qquad \mathbf A=\frac{Br}{2}\hat{\boldsymbol\theta}, \]

the conserved angular momentum is \(J_z=-i\hbar\partial_\theta+\hbar\sigma_z/2\). Write

\[ \Phi(r,\theta)=\begin{pmatrix}\Phi_A(r)e^{im\theta}\\ i\Phi_B(r)e^{i(m+1)\theta}\end{pmatrix}. \]

The radial equations are

\[ \left(\partial_r+\frac{r}{2l_B^2}-\frac{m}{r}\right)\Phi_A =-\frac{E+\Delta}{\hbar v_F}\Phi_B, \]

\[ \left(\partial_r+\frac{m+1}{r}-\frac{r}{2l_B^2}\right)\Phi_B =\frac{E-\Delta}{\hbar v_F}\Phi_A. \]

Eliminating \(\Phi_B\),

\[ \left[\partial_r^2+\frac1r\partial_r+\frac{m+1}{l_B^2} -\frac{r^2}{4l_B^4}-\frac{m^2}{r^2}+q^2\right]\Phi_A=0, \qquad q^2=\frac{E^2-\Delta^2}{(\hbar v_F)^2}. \]

The regular solutions are

\[ \Phi_A^+(r)=\mathcal N_+r^m e^{-r^2/(4l_B^2)} {}_1F_1\!\left(-\frac{l_B^2q^2}{2},m+1,\frac{r^2}{2l_B^2}\right),\quad m\ge0, \]

\[ \Phi_A^-(r)=\mathcal N_-r^{|m|} e^{-r^2/(4l_B^2)} {}_1F_1\!\left(-m-\frac{l_B^2q^2}{2},1-m,\frac{r^2}{2l_B^2}\right),\quad m<0. \]

The lower component follows from

\[ \Phi_B=-\frac{\hbar v_F}{E+\Delta} \left(\partial_r+\frac{r}{2l_B^2}-\frac{m}{r}\right)\Phi_A. \]

Partial-wave expansion

An incident massless Dirac plane wave moving along \(x\) has the expansion

\[ \Phi^i_k(r,\theta)=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}e^{ikr\cos\theta} \begin{pmatrix}1\\1\end{pmatrix} =\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\sum_{m=-\infty}^{\infty}i^m \begin{pmatrix}J_m(kr)e^{im\theta}\\ iJ_{m+1}(kr)e^{i(m+1)\theta}\end{pmatrix}. \]

The outgoing wave is

\[ \Phi^r_k(r,\theta)=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}\sum_{m=-\infty}^{\infty}a_m^r i^m \begin{pmatrix}H_m^{(1)}(kr)e^{im\theta}\\ iH_{m+1}^{(1)}(kr)e^{i(m+1)\theta}\end{pmatrix}. \]

The coefficients \(a_m^r\) are fixed by matching the spinor across the radius of the circular scatterer.
