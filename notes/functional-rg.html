Working note

Functional renormalization group notes

A cleaned technical note on fermionic fRG, the Katanin substitution, and spin functional RG deformation schemes.

Status: background / convention audit needed before manuscript use.

Purpose

These notes collect two related but distinct pieces of functional-renormalization-group machinery: first, the standard fermionic fRG setup with a cutoff-dependent Gaussian part, a single-scale propagator, and vertex/self-energy flow equations; second, the spin fRG formulation for an anisotropic Heisenberg model, where longitudinal and transverse spin couplings are deformed separately.

Use with care. This is a working derivation note, not a polished review article. The main items requiring a later line-by-line audit are signs, regulator conventions, factors of $\beta N$, and the precise meaning of the Katanin replacement in each truncation.

1. Fermionic fRG: minimal setup

Start from a Grassmann functional integral

$$ \mathcal Z = \int \mathcal D(\bar\psi,\psi)\, e^{-S(\bar\psi,\psi)} . $$

Separate the quadratic and interacting parts as

$$ S(\bar\psi,\psi)=(\bar\psi,Q\psi)-\widetilde V(\bar\psi,\psi), \qquad (\bar\psi,Q\psi)=\sum_j \bar\psi_j Q_j \psi_j . $$

For a translationally invariant fermion problem the composite index is typically $j=(i\omega_n,\mathbf k,s)$ and $Q_j=i\omega_n-\xi_{\mathbf k}$, with $\xi_{\mathbf k}=\varepsilon_{\mathbf k}-\mu$. The inverse of the quadratic kernel is the bare propagator, $G_0=Q^{-1}$, up to the convention used for signs and normalization.

2. Cutoff and single-scale propagator

A cutoff-dependent quadratic kernel can be introduced by

$$ Q^\Lambda = Q/\chi(\Lambda), \qquad \chi(\Lambda)\approx \Theta(|\xi|-\Lambda). $$

The interpretation is that modes with $|\xi|>\Lambda$ have been integrated in. The flow starts near the bandwidth scale $\Lambda_i\sim W$ and ends at $\Lambda_f=0$, where $\chi(0)=1$.

The full propagator is

$$ G^\Lambda = \left(Q^\Lambda-\Sigma^\Lambda\right)^{-1}. $$

The single-scale propagator is the derivative of $G^\Lambda$ at fixed self-energy:

$$ S^\Lambda =-G^\Lambda\,\dot Q^\Lambda\,G^\Lambda, \qquad \dot Q^\Lambda\equiv \partial_\Lambda Q^\Lambda . $$

In a sharp-shell scheme, $\dot\chi$ produces a delta function on the running shell $|\xi_{\mathbf k}|=\Lambda$.

3. One-loop flow and Katanin replacement

At the usual one-loop truncation level, the self-energy and two-particle vertex obey closed flow equations after higher vertices are either neglected or approximated. Diagrammatically, the self-energy flow contains one single-scale line and one two-particle vertex, while the two-particle vertex flow contains particle-particle and particle-hole loop corrections.

The Katanin replacement promotes the single-scale structure in the two-particle flow according to

$$ S^\Lambda G^\Lambda + G^\Lambda S^\Lambda \quad\longrightarrow\quad \partial_\Lambda\left(G^\Lambda G^\Lambda\right). $$

Equivalently, inside the corresponding two-particle loop, this accounts for the self-energy feedback term contained in $\partial_\Lambda G^\Lambda$. This is often essential when the flow is used to approach symmetry-broken regimes.

Convention check. Do not globally replace every $S^\Lambda$ by $\partial_\Lambda G^\Lambda$ without checking the truncation. The Katanin substitution is a controlled modification of the two-particle flow, not a magic rule for every line in every diagram.

4. Useful summation trick

Momentum sums can often be reduced to energy integrals of the form

$$ \sum_{\mathbf k} \longrightarrow \int_{-W}^{W} \dd\varepsilon\,\rho(\varepsilon). $$

If the scale dependence enters only through $\chi$ and $\Sigma$, then for an integrand $\mathcal I(\xi,\chi,\Sigma)$,

$$ \int_{-W}^{W}\dd\varepsilon\,\rho(\varepsilon)\,\partial_\Lambda\mathcal I =\int_{-W}^{W}\dd\varepsilon\,\rho(\varepsilon) \left( \dot\chi\,\frac{\partial\mathcal I}{\partial\chi} +\dot\Sigma\,\frac{\partial\mathcal I}{\partial\Sigma} \right). $$

For a sharp cutoff this becomes a shell contribution plus self-energy feedback:

$$ \int\dd\varepsilon\,\rho(\varepsilon) \left[ \left.\mathcal I\right|_{\chi=1}\delta(|\varepsilon|-\Lambda) +\dot\Sigma\,\frac{\partial\mathcal I}{\partial\Sigma} \right]. $$

5. Spin fRG deformation scheme

For the anisotropic Heisenberg model, write

$$ \mathcal H=\mathcal H_0+\mathcal V, $$ $$ \mathcal V=-\frac12\sum_{ij} \left[J_{ij}^{\perp}\left(S_i^+S_j^-+S_i^-S_j^+\right) +J_{ij}^{z}S_i^zS_j^z\right]. $$

The deformation parameter $\Lambda$ is introduced through scale-dependent couplings

$$ J_{\Lambda,ij}^{\perp}=J_{ij}^{\perp}-R_{\Lambda,ij}^{\perp}, \qquad J_{\Lambda,ij}^{z}=J_{ij}^{z}-R_{\Lambda,ij}^{z}. $$

The regulator is chosen so that the initial point is solvable and the final point recovers the physical model. The scheme may simply switch on the interaction, or it may deform the range of the interaction.

6. Generating functional and Wetterich-type structure

With source fields $\mathbf h_i(\tau)$, define

$$ \mathcal G_\Lambda[\mathbf h] =\ln\Tr\left[e^{-\beta\mathcal H_0} \mathcal T\exp\left\{\int_0^\beta\dd\tau \left[\sum_i\mathbf h_i(\tau)\cdot\mathbf S_i(\tau)-\mathcal V_\Lambda(\tau)\right] \right\}\right]. $$

Connected spin correlators follow by source differentiation:

$$ G_{\Lambda,I_1\cdots I_n}^{\alpha_1\cdots\alpha_n} =\left. \frac{\delta^n\mathcal G_\Lambda[\mathbf h]} {\delta h_{I_1}^{\bar\alpha_1}\cdots\delta h_{I_n}^{\bar\alpha_n}} \right|_{h=0}. $$

After a modified Legendre transform, the irreducible functional satisfies a formal one-loop exact equation

$$ \partial_\Lambda\mathcal L_\Lambda[\mathbf M] =\frac12\Tr\left[ \left(\mathcal L_\Lambda''[\mathbf M]+\mathcal R_\Lambda\right)^{-1} \partial_\Lambda\mathcal R_\Lambda \right]. $$

This is the spin analogue of a Wetterich equation, with the caveat that the spin variables are not ordinary canonical bosonic fields and the longitudinal/transverse sectors carry different bookkeeping.

7. Hybrid functional and longitudinal amputation

A useful construction decouples the longitudinal interaction using a Hubbard-Stratonovich field $\eta$ and introduces a source $s$ for that field. The resulting hybrid functional is

$$ \mathcal F_\Lambda[\mathbf h^\perp,s] =\mathcal G_\Lambda\left[\mathbf h^\perp, h_i^z=\sum_jJ_{\Lambda,ij}^zs_j\right] +\frac12\sum_{IJ}\mathbb J^z_{\Lambda,IJ}s_Is_J. $$

Its derivatives define correlation functions that are connected in the transverse sector and amputated in the longitudinal sector. For example,

$$ F_{\Lambda,I}^{z}=\sum_J\mathbb J^z_{\Lambda,IJ}G_{\Lambda,J}^{z}, $$ $$ F_{\Lambda,IJ}^{zz} =\sum_{KL}\mathbb J^z_{\Lambda,IK}\mathbb J^z_{\Lambda,JL}G_{\Lambda,KL}^{zz} +\mathbb J^z_{\Lambda,IJ}. $$

8. Symmetry-broken phase and exchange field

In the ordered phase the magnetization is taken along the $z$ direction,

$$ \langle S_I^\alpha\rangle_\Lambda=M_\Lambda\delta^{\alpha z}. $$

In the hybrid representation the corresponding longitudinal exchange field is

$$ \bar\Phi_{I,\Lambda}^\alpha=\phi_\Lambda\delta^{\alpha z}, \qquad \phi_\Lambda=J^z_{\Lambda,0}M_\Lambda. $$

The Legendre transform to a vertex functional $\Gamma_\Lambda[\Phi]$ is then expanded about this running extremum. This avoids a spurious linear term in the fluctuation fields provided $\phi_\Lambda$ is flowed consistently.

9. Tree-level two-point identities

In momentum-frequency space, $K=(\mathbf k,i\omega)$, the transverse and longitudinal two-point functions obey

$$ F_\Lambda^{+-}(K)=\left[\Gamma_\Lambda^{+-}(K)+R_{\Lambda,k}^{\perp}\right]^{-1}, $$ $$ F_\Lambda^{zz}(K)=\left[\Gamma_\Lambda^{zz}(K)+R_{\Lambda,k}^{\phi}\right]^{-1}. $$

The transverse connected and partially amputated functions coincide,

$$ G_\Lambda(K)=G_\Lambda^{+-}(K)=F_\Lambda^{+-}(K), $$

while the longitudinal relation contains the exchange-line amputation,

$$ F_\Lambda(K)=F_\Lambda^{zz}(K)=\left(J^z_{\Lambda,k}\right)^2G_\Lambda^{zz}(K)+J^z_{\Lambda,k}. $$

Therefore,

$$ \Gamma_\Lambda^{+-}(K)=G_\Lambda^{-1}(K)+J_{\Lambda,k}^{\perp}-J_k^{\perp}, $$ $$ \Gamma_\Lambda^{zz}(K) =-\frac{G_\Lambda^{zz}(K)}{1+J^z_{\Lambda,k}G_\Lambda^{zz}(K)}+\frac{1}{J_k^z}. $$

10. Vertex-flow hierarchy

Expanding the exact functional flow in powers of the fields produces an infinite hierarchy of coupled integro-differential equations for the vertices $\Gamma_{\Lambda,K_1\cdots K_n}^{\alpha_1\cdots\alpha_n}$.

The free-energy flow is schematically

$$ \partial_\Lambda f_\Lambda =\int_KG_\Lambda(K)\partial_\Lambda R^\perp_{\Lambda,k} +\frac12\int_K\left[F_\Lambda(K)-J^z_{\Lambda,k}\right]\partial_\Lambda R^\phi_{\Lambda,k} -\frac12\phi_\Lambda^2\partial_\Lambda R^\phi_{\Lambda,0}. $$

The exchange-field flow is fixed by demanding that the fluctuation expansion contain no linear term:

$$ \Gamma_\Lambda^{zz}(0)\partial_\Lambda\phi_\Lambda =-\partial_\Lambda\left(R^\phi_{\Lambda,0}\phi_\Lambda\right) -\int_K\dot G_\Lambda(K)\Gamma_\Lambda^{+-z}(K,-K,0) -\frac12\int_K\dot F_\Lambda(K)\Gamma_\Lambda^{zzz}(K,-K,0). $$

The transverse and longitudinal single-scale propagators are

$$ \dot G_\Lambda(K)=-G_\Lambda^2(K)\partial_\Lambda R^\perp_{\Lambda,k}, \qquad \dot F_\Lambda(K)=-F_\Lambda^2(K)\partial_\Lambda R^\phi_{\Lambda,k}. $$

11. Infinite-range / Katanin benchmark

The original note also attempted a benchmark in an infinite-range limit, comparing a Katanin-corrected flow against a tree-level vertex-corrected flow. The relevant self-consistency structure is the running gap

$$ \Delta_\Lambda=H\frac{M_0}{M_\Lambda}, $$

together with a flow for the magnetization of the schematic form

$$ \partial_\Lambda M_\Lambda =\frac{M_0}{\beta N}\sum_{\mathbf k,\omega} \frac{\Gamma^{+-z}_{\Lambda,\mathrm{Kat}}\delta(k-\Lambda)} {\Delta_\Lambda+E_{\mathbf k}-i\omega}. $$

After the Matsubara/momentum integrations, the notes reduce this to a scalar flow for $M_\Lambda$ and compare the Katanin result with a vertex-corrected approximation.

Audit needed. This benchmark is the least polished part of the note. The scaling statement for the susceptibility and the $H\to0$ limit should be rederived from scratch before being reused.
