Exercises and derivations

Running physics problems

A public list of compact physics problems, derivations, and technical checks. The goal is to improve fluency with field theory, topology, quantum mechanics, and condensed-matter methods. This is not a list of claimed original research problems.

How to use this page

Each item is meant to be small enough to attack with pencil-and-paper work, but serious enough to expose a useful physical or mathematical mechanism. I keep private research directions, unpublished calculations, and implementation details off this public page.

Problem list

Berry curvature of a two-level Hamiltonian
Area: geometric phases, two-band models. Status: exercise.

Let \(H(\mathbf{k})=\mathbf{d}(\mathbf{k})\cdot\boldsymbol{\sigma}\), with \(\hat{\mathbf d}=\mathbf d/|\mathbf d|\). Show that the Berry curvature of an isolated band is proportional to the solid angle swept by \(\hat{\mathbf d}(\mathbf{k})\) on \(S^2\). In coordinates, derive the standard form \(F_{ij}=\pm \frac12\,\hat{\mathbf d}\cdot (\partial_i\hat{\mathbf d}\times\partial_j\hat{\mathbf d})\).

Screening of the background electric field in the \(CP^{N-1}\) model
Area: 1+1D field theory, theta angle, confinement. Status: reading problem.

In the \(CP^{N-1}\) model, review the large-\(N\) picture in which the theta angle acts like a background electric field. Explain what happens near \(\theta=\pi\), how charged \(z\) quanta screen the background field, and how this is reflected in the branch structure of the vacuum energy.

Spin\(_c\) connections in \(2+1\)-dimensional Chern-Simons theory
Area: Chern-Simons theory, spin structures, anomalies. Status: conceptual derivation.

Clarify the distinction between an ordinary \(U(1)\) connection and a Spin\(_c\) connection in \(2+1\) dimensions. Work out how this distinction enters the quantization of Chern-Simons terms and the coupling of background gauge fields to fermionic systems.

Particle on a ring with threaded flux
Area: quantum mechanics, instantons, topology. Status: exercise.

Compute the partition function of a particle on a ring threaded by flux by summing over winding sectors. Show explicitly how integer flux can be gauged away, while half-integer flux produces a twofold ground-state degeneracy.

Fermi-surface deformation and a Pomeranchuk instability
Area: Fermi liquids, symmetry breaking. Status: exercise to expand.

Starting from Landau's energy functional, derive the criterion for a Pomeranchuk instability in an angular-momentum channel. Then specialize to a two-dimensional quadrupolar distortion and identify the nematic order parameter.
